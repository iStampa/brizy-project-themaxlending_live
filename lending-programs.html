Industry Terms

Adjustable Rate Mortgage (ARM)

A mortgage in which the interest rate is adjusted periodically based on a preselected index. Also sometimes known as a renegotiable rate mortgage or variable rate mortgage.

Adjustment Date

The date that the interest rate changes on an adjustable rate mortgage (ARM)

Amortization

Loan payment divided into equal periodic payments calculated to pay off the debt at the end of a fixed period including accrued interest on the outstanding balance.

Amortization Term

The length of time required to amortize the mortgage loan expressed as a number of months. For example, 360 months is the amortization term for a 30-year fixed rate mortgage.

Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

The measurement of the full cost of a loan including interest and loan fees expressed as a yearly percentage rate. Because all the lenders apply the same rules in calculating the annual percentage rate it provides consumers with a good basis for comparing the cost of different loans.

Charis Abrams

Principal / CEO / Founder

In the 2008 financial crash, Charis closed her Utah operation and moved to Las Vegas. Seeing opportunity in REO, Charis quickly got her real estate brokers license and became the managing broker of one of the valley's largest independent brokerages.

During this time, Charis personally sold over 3,000 SFR for her banking clients and traded over $900,000,000 NPL trades making her one of the top producing female real estate agents in the country.

Charis differs from a traditional mortgage broker or real estate agent in many respects. Between 2010- 2014, Charis was the architect and President of a real estate software platform. That platform was the first to take a defaulting note from first pay default all the way through REO disposition and much more.

In 2012, she created a module that allowed her company to be the first software to analyze SFR in mass nationwide. This allowed groups such as Progress Residential to have actionable data on over $16,000,000,000 in SFR assets across 17 markets in 6 months.

The software business gave her the unique experience of sitting at the intersection of Bank/ Investor, Servicer, Acquisition Fund, Escrow/ Title company and Broker. "I wasn't there for just one sale; I was there to listen to each stake holder and implement their needs into our software." This gave her a deep understanding of each of their processes and how to maneuver between them.

This in-depth knowledge of not only mortgage, but all of the supporting industries is what led her to the NON-QM mortgage space and to build a company not just for now but for the future.

Jason Abrams

President

Jason Abrams is a serial entrepreneur. He started his first business while on active duty in the US Marine Corps in Honolulu, HI. His production and promotion company was the first to bring hip hop and dance music to the nightlife of Waikiki.

After a successful 3 year run the effects of the Nikkei stock index crash took its toll and the economy's primary drive, Asian tourism, finally died on the islands.

Wanting to pivot quickly, Jason set his sights on the entertainment capital of the world, Las Vegas. Jason was quick to notice the staggering boom of the Las Vegas Real Estate market and jumped right in. Over the next 20+ years, Jason successfully brokered approximately 8,500 single family home sales, making him one of the top producing agents in the country.

After the REO cycle, Jason felt the need to venture into a different genre of real estate. Having an eye for architecture and design and having a unique knowledge of how to buy properties right, Jason went headfirst into home flipping. After completing several flips, he wanted to do something special. Growing up in Los Angeles and living in a historic neighborhood of Las Vegas allowed him to see Las Vegas as an up-and-coming untapped gem. Jason started buying houses by famed mid-century modern architect team Palmer and Krisel and adopted the motto "if I build it, they will come".

He started dramatically over-improving these properties, believing in his vision of what that area could become. The bet paid off, and buyers flocked to his houses paying up to $100,000 over appraisal, a big deal for $400,000 homes. Jason's next move was onto the luxury market, where Jason's homes continue to break every pricing record in Las Vegas. His most recent sale sold for a million dollars over market value.

The lending business was the natural progression for Jason. The largest portion of his 8,500 sales were investor and REO. Jason now utilizes his national database of investors, natural tenacity and knowledge of home flipping to speak to borrowers in their language and gain their trust, which he values tremendously.
